Educate and Empower with Healthy Eating

Here at little chefs, we give kids and families the opportunity to get creative in the kitchen through hands-on learning with fresh and affordable ingredients.

Cooking allows kids to explore food with all their senses and experience the satisfaction of self-made meals.

Our Story

Little Chefs is a website where kids teach kids to cook. Our main goals are to foster peer-to-peer learning, healthy eating, and teaching children life skills. The roots of Little Chefs begin in the late 1980's on the set of a National Geographic shoot for a film introducing the new food pyramid to school kids. The consultants thought that a pizza with vegetables, meat and cheese would be in line with the recommendations of the food pyramid, and would be something a kid might want to eat. The idea was sparked when after the shoot was finished, the young, eight year old actor who had just completed making the pizza turned to me and said, "I had no idea pizza didn't come out of a box". It seems impossible today when 10 year old chefs are competing against professionals on television but that was a time when the market was still being dominated by fast food and convenience food for children. The writing style of Little Chefs was designed for latch-key kids. So while writing recipes, I went to great lengths to make sure a child working alone could figure out what to do. Additionally, I wanted to counter the culture of childrens cookbooks utilizing sweets and junk food to appeal to their audience by using healthy and family friendly recipes.
